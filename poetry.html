The Collected Haiku

A chronological archive of poems by Tuan Tran

This archive contains all haiku written by Tuan Tran to date.

The first fifteen poems appeared on tuantran.org in June 2001. The remaining, except poem 16, were written between May 2026 and now, presented in chronological order.

Last updated: June 16, 2026

1
I saw
an earthworm
crawling
next to my feet
carefully
I picked it up
and placed it
in a flowerpot
so I wouldn't step on it
then I saw
another
I placed it
in the same flowerpot
so the other
won't be lonely
2
Like a sailboat
in a vast blue sea
without a compass
or a star
to point the way
then you appear
bright as the northern star
radiant and lovely
with that far-away look
in your eyes
and lips so moist
as the morning dew
and suddenly
I am no longer lost
as I steer
by the light
of the northern star
3
Spiderweb
collecting morning dew
a graveyard of mosquitoes
4
Dark clouds
approaching
songbirds still sing
5
Desert moon
shone brightly
a lone wanderer
6
Dragonfly
in a pond of lotus flowers
be still
7
Bamboo branches swaying in the gentle breeze
water buffaloes reflecting in the moonlight
bombs falling like raindrops
8
Ego
my own ego
big as a mountain
chip at it
a little
each day
9
Master
seeking for you
halfway around the world
Guru
where are you
but in my heart
10
Ten years
seems as yesterday
I have not let go
11
Autumn breeze
falling leaves
why think of summer
12
Every caterpillar
metamorphoses
in its own time
13
How strange
dragonflies
spitting fire
14
One boat
alone
this vast ocean
15
In my darkest hour
I cried out for you
I love you, mom
16
Temple bell ringing
I think
of my friend
